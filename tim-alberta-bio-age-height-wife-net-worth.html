In 2019, Tim headed back to Michigan. Instead of just covering the 2020 campaign like everyone else, he hit the road, reporting from gun shows, farmers markets, diverse gatherings, and different neighborhoods. He penned regular “Letters to Washington” that didn’t dive into typical politics.

Instead, he explored deeper issues: disappearing communities, fading trust in vital stuff, cultural clashes, economic gaps between rich and regular folks, America’s changing face, and the rise of racism and xenophobia. By March 2021, Tim joined The Atlantic with a mission: keep traveling, keep writing, and keep telling stories that hit Americans right in the feels.

His work’s been all over the place, from Sports Illustrated to Vanity Fair. You might’ve caught him on TV too, sharing his thoughts not just in the US, but all around the world. His book, “American Carnage,” was a hit, ranking high on best-seller lists. Tim’s based in southeast Michigan with his wife, three sons, and trusty German Shepherd.
