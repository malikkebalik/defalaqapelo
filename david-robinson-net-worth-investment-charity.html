Career

Collegiate Career

Robinson was a basketball player at the United States Naval Academy in Annapolis, Maryland.

Further, he led the team to the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) tournament in 1985, 1986, and 1987.

Even though Robinson was one of the best college players in the country, some analysts considered it a gamble when the Spurs selected him with the first pick in the 1987 NBA draft.

Besides, he had to serve two years of intensive duties and responsibilities in the United States Navy after graduation.

NBA Career

David Robinson joined the San Antonio Spurs in 1989 after finishing his naval time.

During his first season, he immediately established himself as among the greatest centers in the NBA, averaging 24.3 points, 12 rebounds, and 3.9 blocked shots per game.

As a consequence, he was named NBA Rookie of the Year in 1989.

Furthermore, he assisted the Spurs in winning 35 more games than the previous season, which was the biggest one-year increase in league history at the time.

The next season, Robinson kept playing exceptionally well, earning the All-NBA first team and the All-NBA defensive first team.

Robinson received individual honors throughout his early career, including being named the NBA’s Defensive Player of the Year in 1992 and Most Valuable Player in 1995.

Moreover, he was named to the league’s official list of the 50 greatest players in NBA history (1996).

In the 1998–99 season when Robinson and second-year power forward Tim Duncan led the Spurs to their first NBA title.

Sadly, Robinson retired after the 2002–03 season, following which the Spurs won their second NBA title.

David Robinson Charity

Basketball Hall of Famer David Robinson is a compassionate, kind-hearted person and down-to-earth guy.

In addition to his successful NBA career, Robinson and his wife Valerie established the David Robinson Foundation in November 1992.

Robinson’s charity endeavors are as impressive as his on-court accomplishments.

Beginning in 1991 at San Antonio’s Gates Elementary School, he gave a $2,000 scholarship to each and every fifth-grade student who graduated from high school and went on to college.

David is still involved with IDEA and its ambitions to develop twenty additional schools in the San Antonio area.

In 2000, he was honored with the Patriot Award, the highest honor bestowed by the Congressional Medal of Honor Society for their effort and dedication towards society.

moreover, the David Robinson Foundation donated about $3 million in total for numerous Covid-19 relief funds in San Antonio.

Likewise, his foundation also provided safety gear such as face shields, PPE suits, masks, Oxygen cylinders, and ventilators.
