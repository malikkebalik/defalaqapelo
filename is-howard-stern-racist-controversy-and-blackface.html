Is Howard Stern Racist?

The question of whether Howard Stern is racist has been a contentious one. The famed radio host, known for his provocative content, faced a backlash when a 1993 television special resurfaced.

There, he appeared in blackface and used racially charged language. In this sketch, Stern and his longtime co-host Robin Quivers satirized a Friar’s Club Roast of Whoopi Goldberg, where Ted Danson had controversially appeared in blackface.

Stern addressed the controversy on his SiriusXM show: “The shot I did was f**ing crazy. I’ll be the first to admit… But that was my shtick, that’s what I did, and I own it.”

Stern added that he would not repeat such actions if he had the chance to do it over again. The controversy has sparked a broader discussion on Stern’s past content and his evolution as a host.

Stern has been on the radio for nearly four decades. Likewise, he acknowledged that he cringes when looking back at his content from 30 or 40 years ago.

He stated that he has evolved and toned down his show since beginning therapy, indicating a shift in his approach to his content and its potential to offend. Robin Quivers, who was called the N-word by Stern in the 1993 sketch, defended him.

He emphasized Stern’s evolution and long-standing support for free speech, women’s rights, and the LGBTQ cause.

Howard Stern Controversy And Blackface

The controversy around Stern’s blackface performance comes during heightened racial sensitivities and a renewed focus on representation and historical racial injustices in the media.

Blackface was a form of theatrical makeup used predominantly by non-black performers. It was to represent a caricature of a black person.

Stern’s satirical skit was a response to a similar incident involving Ted Danson at a Friar’s Club Roast of Whoopi Goldberg. Although Stern intended to lampoon Danson’s actions, his choice to don blackface while doing so has been criticized.

While Stern did not formally apologize for the skit, he admitted that his approach was wrong and said he would not do it again. However, the controversy has led to a debate about the nature of comedy, its boundaries, and its potential to offend.
