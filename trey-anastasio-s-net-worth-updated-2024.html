Early Life 

David Adedeji Adeleke was born November 21, 1992, in Atlanta, Georgia, United States.

His mother, Vero Adeleke, was a university lecturer, while his father, Adedeji Adeleke, is a rich Nigerian businessman.

Davido, the second son born to his father and the youngest of five children, attended the British International School in Lagos.

He returned to the United States at the age of 16 to enroll at Oakwood University in Alabama to study business administration.

While attending Oakwood, Davido acquired musical instruments and began producing beats, cofounding the musical group KB International with his cousins B-Red and Sina Rambo.

Davido relocated to London to improve his vocals after quitting Oakwood University to devote himself to music full-time.

To respect his father, Davido decided to suspend his music career after moving back to Nigeria in 2011 and enrolled at Babcock University.

He received a music degree from Babcock in July 2015 after his father sponsored the school to establish a music department for a single-student inaugural class.

The album was released in 2012 and got unfavorable reviews from music critics; however, it was nominated for Album of the Year at The Headies in 2013 and won Best R&B/Pop Album.
